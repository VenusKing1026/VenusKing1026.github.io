M6A review

m6A 's basic molecular function, function in chromatin and genome integrity and development and differentiation

Jun 08, 2025
2 minute read

1. Definition and basic concepts

![[Pasted image 20250608143245.png]]

  • ~25% of mammalian mRNAs contain m6A
  • m6A was found in UTR3’ and near stop codons of transcripts that regulate differentiation and development
  • best characterized protein family which binds to m6A: YTH family

2. Basic molecular function (fig 1,2)

  • 2.1 pre-RNA splicing and nuclear export

    • 2.1.1 m6A affact alternative splicing : a.directly b.indirectly

      • a.directly: recruiting SRSF3 –> exon retention
      • b.indirect: exposing - HNRNPC –> exon retention

    • 2.1.2 m6A facilitate shuttling of mRNAs to the cytoplasm for translation

  • 2.2 mRNA degradation and stability

    • **strong context dependence
    • ==futrue work== need to determine what additional factor determine whether the transcript need to degradate or stablize

  • 2.3 mRNA translation

    • 5’ UTR m6A: initiation (cap-indepedent)
    • 3’ UTR: enhance translation (cap-dependent)
    • fact shows m6A can inhibit tRNA interaction thereby affacting elongatioin rate

  • 2.4 refines ribosome binding

    • only 2 conserved position are methylated:
      • 18s rRNA:1832
      • 28s rRNA:4220
